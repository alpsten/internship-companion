# Content Outline

A structured snapshot of the early brainstorming notes. Each section pairs on-page copy with supporting assets (templates, downloads, checklists).

## 1. Intro & Mindset
- Opening message explaining why internships feel unclear and how this site helps.
- Key promise: reduce stress, stay organised, land the one "yes" you need.
- Mantras: build connections, embrace small steps, celebrate persistence.

## 2. Ground Rules
- Personalise every outreach; show you understand the company.
- Follow up respectfully (once or twice) without spamming.
- Stay polite, humble, and thankful regardless of the response.
- Be consistent: small daily actions > irregular bursts.

## 3. Preparation Guides
### 3.1 LinkedIn
- Explain purpose of LinkedIn for LIA.
- Checklist: photo, headline (e.g., "Java Developer Student | Looking for Internship (LIA) Spring [year]"), experience, featured projects, summary.
- Encourage networking before internship season; compare English vs Swedish profiles.

### 3.2 GitHub
- Why GitHub matters and how to set it up (professional username, tidy repos).
- Guidance on documentation, README quality, licenses, clear commit messages.
- Recommend pinning 2–3 polished projects; remove throwaway repos.

### 3.3 CV & Portfolio
- Structure (education-first unless strong work history).
- Suggest canvas/template options and supplementary channels (Instagram dev account, personal site).

## 4. Leverage Your Network
- Remind users they already have a network (family, friends, classmates, teachers, alumni, colleagues).
- Tips on extending the chain and logging who they contact.
- Emphasise helping each other and tracking progress.

## 5. Contacting Employers
- Purpose: show genuine interest with concise outreach.
- Email template guidance (5–7 sentences, subject line, introduce self, link portfolio, avoid attachments).
- LinkedIn messaging tips (premium? keep short, find the right contact).
- Calling script pointers: prepare pitch, take notes, ask who to talk to.
- Dropping by on-site: dress appropriately, bring CV, respect their time.
- Following up: send a polite reminder a week later, be thankful, keep doors open.

## 6. Interview Preparation
- Frame interviews as conversations, not interrogations.
- Behavioural reminders: be yourself, stay curious and humble.
- Common questions (tell me about yourself, why us, strengths/weaknesses, proud project).
- Encourage STAR method stories and tailored examples from school/work.
- Questions for the candidate to ask (daily life, technologies, values in interns).
- Reflection checklist after interviews.

## 7. Handling Rejection
- Normalize rejection; treat it as training.
- Steps: say thank you, request feedback, keep interest for future roles.
- Reflection prompts and momentum boosters (review messages, celebrate small wins).

## 8. Goal Setting & Tracking
- Break the search into milestones (update profiles, build company list, send first contacts, follow up, prep interviews).
- Encourage regular logging: number of applications, replies, interviews, streaks.
- Suggest visual trackers (progress bar, streak counter) and growth mindset reminders.

## 9. Resources & Downloads
- Templates & trackers (spreadsheets, PDFs, checklists).
- Message templates (email, LinkedIn, call scripts).
- Guides & articles (in-depth explainers, inspirational pieces).
- Reflection & mindset prompts.
- Future automated resources to showcase technical skills (e.g., generators, scheduling helpers).

## 10. FAQ
- "When should I start applying?" → As early as possible.
- "What if I don’t get an internship?" → Stay persistent; reinforce belief in success.
- "I don’t have a portfolio" → Start small; quality over quantity.
- "Nobody is answering" → Try again, reach out to different companies, align with interests.
- "I’m late to the party" → Ask around; leverage network.

## Notes for Implementation
- Use collapsible sections/accordions to prevent walls of text.
- Highlight quotes and mantras visually to keep motivation high.
- Pair each major section with actionable CTA (download template, tick checklist, log outreach).
- Prepare Swedish copy first; collect English translations in parallel for later toggle.
