# Product Brief

## Context
Many Swedish software students must complete a LIA (internship) to graduate. The process feels unstructured, stressful, and opaque, especially when students juggle coursework with outreach, interview prep, and follow-ups. The Internship Companion project should give them a calm, actionable companion they can trust.

## Audience
- **Primary:** Swedish-speaking developer students searching for a LIA placement in the next 3–9 months.
- **Secondary:** Teachers, mentors, or classmates who want to share a clear playbook.

## Problem Statement
Students struggle to plan and execute their internship search. They often burn time rewriting messages, misplacing leads, and losing momentum after rejection. They need guidance that balances motivation with concrete next steps.

## Vision & Value Proposition
Provide a single hub that:
- Structures the search into manageable steps (prepare, reach out, follow up, interview, reflect).
- Supplies high-quality Swedish templates, trackers, and checklists that students can customise quickly.
- Reinforces the mindset that persistence and professionalism matter more than perfection.

## Guiding Principles
1. **Personal but scalable:** Encourage tailoring messages, never blind copy-paste.
2. **Consistency over intensity:** Promote daily or weekly routines, not one-off binges.
3. **Professional empathy:** Model politeness, gratitude, and resilience throughout the process.
4. **Clarity first:** Every page should make it obvious what to do next.

## Core Journeys
1. **Prepare your presence** – polish LinkedIn, GitHub, CV, and supporting channels.
2. **Map your network** – identify who you already know and how to extend that chain.
3. **Reach out effectively** – craft emails, LinkedIn messages, and call scripts; know when to follow up.
4. **Ace the interview** – rehearse common questions, plan questions to ask, capture takeaways.
5. **Handle rejection & keep momentum** – reflect, learn, and move to the next opportunity.
6. **Track goals** – set weekly targets, log outreach, monitor replies, celebrate streaks.

## MVP Scope
- Static Swedish-first content for each journey with collapsible sections to avoid walls of text.
- Downloadable assets: spreadsheet tracker, editable text templates, printable checklist, calendar reminder packs.
- Simple progress indicators (e.g., checkbox list or streak counter) stored locally.
- Responsive frontend that can be exported as static HTML for GitHub Pages.

## Future Enhancements
- English localisation toggle or separate build.
- Interactive planner (e.g., progress bar, reminders) with optional backend.
- Automated resource generators (email drafts, weekly reminders, ICS scheduling).
- Integrations with Google Sheets/Notion for syncing trackers.

## Success Indicators (directional)
- Users can complete the “Get ready” onboarding in under 10 minutes.
- Returning visitors use the checklist at least weekly during their search.
- Teachers/mentors share the site as a recommended resource.
- Qualitative feedback: students feel less overwhelmed and more organised.

## Risks & Considerations
- Content must stay current with Swedish hiring norms; plan for periodic reviews.
- Balancing motivational tone with actionable specificity.
- Avoiding scope creep into fully automated tooling before the core content shines.

## Open Questions
- Which downloadable formats resonate most (Google Docs, PDF, Notion, Excel)?
- How much data persistence is needed for MVP (local storage vs. external sync)?
- What cadence should we plan for English localisation work?
