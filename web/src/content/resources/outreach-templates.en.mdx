## How to use the templates
1. Spend 2–3 minutes reading the company site.
2. Pull one concrete detail to mention.
3. Customize the template: replace bracket text and add why you care.

## Email template
```
Subject: Internship (LIA) spring 2025 – [your name]

Hi [name],

I’m [your name], a [program/school] student looking for a spring 2025 internship. I was drawn to your work with [specific initiative/technology].

A little about me:
- [One sentence on your technical strength]
- [One sentence on a project or result]

Here’s my portfolio: [link]. I’d love to chat about how I could contribute.

Thanks for your time!
[Signature]
```

### Follow-up (after 7 business days)
```
Subject: Follow-up – LIA spring 2025

Hi [name],

Just checking whether you’ve had a chance to read my earlier message. I’m still very interested in doing my internship with you—especially because of [detail].

Here is the link again to my portfolio: [link].

Thanks for your time and response!
[Signature]
```

## LinkedIn message
```
Hi [name]! I study [program] and am looking for a spring 2025 internship. Loved your work with [detail]. Would you have time for a quick chat about LIA opportunities?
```

### Quick follow-up on LinkedIn
```
Hi [name], just following up on my previous message. Are you looking for interns this spring? I’d love to contribute with [short strengths].
```

## Phone script (short)
```
Hi! I’m [your name] from [program]. I’m calling to find out who is responsible for LIA interns within [area]. Do you have a minute?
```

## Drop-in checklist
- Pick the right time: Tuesday–Thursday mornings are usually calmer.
- Bring two printed CVs and a small notebook.
- Prepare one sentence about why you like the company.
- If they say no, thank them for their time and ask whom to contact instead.

## FAQ from recruiters
- **“How much supervision do you need?”** – Explain how you ask questions, take initiative, and escalate when stuck.
- **“Have you worked in a team before?”** – Use school projects or group assignments as examples.
- **“What do you want to learn with us?”** – Connect their tech stack with what excites you.
